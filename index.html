Toronto Games Week

Toronto Games Week (TGW) is a new celebration of videogame arts and culture in the city.

The first TGW will be June 1-7th 2023 and is a collective coordination of events organized independently by dozens of organizations, creators, curators, and companies.

What's Going On?

  • We’ll be kicking Toronto Games Week off with a Dirty Rectangles-hosted talk with a visiting indie developer, followed by a social mixer for game creators.
  • The Hand Eye Society will be exhibiting local games in indie arcade cabinets, the Torontrons, in several locations through the city. Chase them down and play them all!
  • Game Workers Unite will be participating in a panel discussion about a new comic book and research paper “If You Don't Like the Game, Change the Rules: Alternative Modes of Videogame Production.”
  • An international curator will be selecting games from York, OCAD and other universities to exhibit in the NextGen Showcase.
  • The City of Toronto is supporting an Indigenous Think Tank and social coordinated by Meagan Byrne.
  • Game Arts International Network is running a three day professional development symposium called GAIA, which will bring 20 game arts curators and organizers from around the world.
  • Collector and preservationist Derek Quenneville will give a talk on various approaches to keeping video game history alive, including: 3D scanning, cultural context, and hands-on play. Attendees will get to try ancient artifacts of Nintendo, including the Game Boy Sewing Machine, Game Boy Camera and Printer, the Family BASIC Keyboard, and the Nintendo Virtual Boy system.
  • Our closing event will be Interactive Ontario’s IO Connect, who will be hosting a Playtesting BBQ featuring unreleased games by Ontario creators, food and drink, and mingling!

Who's doing this?

Each event is independently organized by your favourite local game enthusiasts.

Toronto Games Week is coordinated by Marie LeBlanc Flanagan and Jim Munroe (Game Arts International Network executive director and co-founder of the Hand Eye Society).

TGW came to life with our original group of advisors, a supergroup of game community organizations, including representatives from Toronto Game Jam, Interactive Ontario, Buffer Festival, Vector Festival, DMG, Dirty Rectangles, Hand Eye Society, and Funland Magazine. Additional input was given by representatives of the Ontario Arts Council, OCAD, TIFF, and the City of Toronto IDM Office.
